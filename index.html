Kesha Brooks Founder

A senior product mind with a teacher's instinct.

I've spent my career inside the engine rooms of cloud and AI — leading product as a senior PM at Microsoft, training teams on Copilot, and architecting on AWS and Azure. The pattern I keep seeing is the same one: companies are over-tooled and under-taught.

My independent practice exists for the leaders who already know the tools are powerful — they need someone who can translate capability into culture. That's what I do. With clarity, with rigor, and with the patience of someone who has actually run the workshops, shipped the products, and sat in the meetings.

08 / Q&A Common Questions

The things
everyone asks.

Is this practice independent of your role at Microsoft?
+

Yes. This is an independent advisory and education practice operating outside my Microsoft employment. Engagements are scoped carefully to avoid conflicts of interest and I do not represent Microsoft in any capacity through this work. Views and recommendations are my own.

Do you only train teams that use Microsoft tools?
+

No. I work in vendor-neutral mode. While I have deep Microsoft 365 Copilot and Azure expertise, I'm equally fluent in AWS and have helped clients on multi-cloud journeys. For AI enablement specifically, I work across Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and the open-source landscape — whichever tools your organization actually uses.

What's the smallest engagement you take?
+

A single 90-minute 1:1 strategy session at the Private tier. For teams, the smallest typical engagement is a half-day workshop. I don't take engagements smaller than that because below a certain threshold, the work doesn't actually move outcomes.

How quickly can we start?
+

1:1 coaching sessions can typically begin within 1–2 weeks of a discovery call. Workshops require 4–6 weeks of lead time to do the diagnostic and customize materials properly. Enterprise engagements are usually 6–10 weeks from first conversation to kickoff. Rush timelines available case-by-case.

Do you travel for in-person work?
+

Yes — based in Washington, D.C. and Atlanta with regular travel to both. I work in-person across the U.S. and internationally for engagements that warrant it. Travel and accommodations are typically billed at cost in addition to engagement fees, with rates discussed transparently before contracting.

Do you take advisor or equity arrangements?
+

Selectively, yes. For early-stage companies where my expertise is unusually well-aligned and the relationship is genuinely strategic, I'll consider advisory roles with equity components. These are evaluated case-by-case and never replace my standard engagement structure entirely.

What outcomes do clients typically see?
+

For Copilot enablement: measurable jumps in active usage and surveyed AI fluency within 30–60 days post-engagement. For coaching: clients have moved into Director, Sr. Director, and VP roles within 6–12 months of starting work together. For cloud strategy: clearer architectural decisions, less vendor lock-in panic, more aligned engineering orgs. Specific metrics are agreed on before every engagement.

How do I know which tier is right for me?
+

Book a discovery call. It's free, it's 30 minutes, and you'll leave with a clear recommendation — even if the answer is "you don't actually need me right now." I'd rather under-sell and build a real relationship than oversell and waste both our time.
